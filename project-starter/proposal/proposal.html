The data used in this analysis comes from the wildfire dataset included in the DATAstudio package in R. This dataset compiles records of wildfire events in Portugal, showing the amount of area burned (in hectares) for wildfires in Portugal, and then accompanied by Canadian Forest Fire Weather Index System Indices (CFFWIS). This data was originally collected from the monitoring systems and environmental observation networks. The goal for this project is to investigate how environmental factors influence wildfire behavior, specifically focusing on burned area.

Each row in this dataset represents a day. The dataset includes a primary response variable, burnt_area,which represents the total area burned per day. Since this data is taken on a national level, the analysis focuses on large-scale environmental influences on wildfire activity, rather than individual fire events.

The dataset also includes predictor variables that influence and describe fire weather and fuel conditions:

Fire Weather Index (fwi), Buildup Index (bui), Initial Spread Index (isi), Fine Fuel Moisture Code (ffmc), Duff Moisture Code (dmc), Drought Code (dc), and day, month, year.

3. Possible question and method

From this data, I seek to answer the research question: How do environmental conditions influence the daily total burned area at the national scale?

Supporting Questions:

What is the relationship between FWI and daily burned area? How does ISI affect burned area on active fire days? Does BUI significantly predict the extent of a burned area?

Hypothesized results:

Null hypothesis: Burnt area has no significant relationship with FWI, ISI, BUI, and FFMC

Alternative: Burnt area has a significant relationship with at least one of FWI, ISI, BUI, and FFMC

Methods:
